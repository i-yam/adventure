You can spend years building something nobody wanted. Don't do that.

Customer discovery is one skill split three ways. First you find a problem that is real and not just plausible. Then you learn to hear what people actually mean, under the politeness. Then you look hard at the competition you cannot see. Do them in order. Each one feeds your Class 04 pitch.

The work lives inside the modules below. Pick the first one and start.

Deep dive: why three modules and not one optional

The customer development loop is discovery, then validation, then a pivot when the evidence demands it. The three modules map onto the front of that loop. Finding the problem is discovery. Talking to users is how you validate or kill it. Reading the competition tells you whether solving it would ever have mattered.

You could rush all three in an afternoon. The founders who do usually end up validating the wrong thing, politely, against no one.
