The room attacks the problem, not the solution

03
INTEREST customers / COMFORT no-code / TIME 15min / TRACK core / FEEDS class 04 pitch
The
attack

In Class 04 nobody in the room cares about your solution yet. You have no customers, so there is nothing to defend. What they care about is whether the problem is real. So that is what they come at, and there are three ways they do it.

The first is the vitamin. "That is a nice-to-have. Nobody is bleeding." They are granting that the problem is real but small, the kind people just live with. Your answer is a person who was clearly not living with it, in their own words.

The second is the sample of one. "Sounds like your problem." They are betting your idea is a personal itch you have mistaken for a market. Your answer is other people, not like you, describing the same thing without being led to it.

The third is the workaround. "People already solve this fine, with a spreadsheet and some tape." They are saying the pain is not worth a new thing. Your answer is what the spreadsheet costs them, in hours or errors or the morning it broke.

For each attack you can answer from your fieldwork, you are ready. For each one you cannot answer, you have learned something real, that your research is still thin right there. Better to learn it here than in the room.

The work · 15 minutes
[ NO AI ] DO THIS ONE YOURSELF

Write the three attacks against your own problem, in the room's voice, as mean as they will really be. Under each, write the evidence from your interviews that answers it, with a real quote where you have one. Where you have nothing, leave it blank and circle it. Before Class 04, run one more interview aimed straight at the blank.

You know your interviews. A model does not, so it will invent a rebuttal that sounds right, and a rebuttal that sounds right but is not is exactly what gets you killed in the room.

Deep dive: the attack you cannot answer is the best thing you get today optional

It will not feel that way. It will feel like getting caught. But an objection you cannot answer is free information about where your idea is thin, handed to you before you spend a year building the wrong thing.

The founders who do well in these rooms write the killer question on a card and go find the answer. The ones who do badly spend the week rehearsing why the question was unfair. Nobody in the room cares that it was unfair.
